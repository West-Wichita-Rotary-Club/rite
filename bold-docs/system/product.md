# Product System

Product Owner TL;DR: RITE is a bilingual living documentary and community platform for preserving a 30-year teacher exchange while helping alumni, schools, clubs, host families, and program leaders stay connected.

## Purpose

The platform preserves the Rotary Inter-country Teacher Exchange history and turns it into a living community archive. It should support public discovery, member networking, story contribution, resource sharing, and institutional continuity for future Rotary teacher exchanges.

## Core Audiences

- Teachers: past participants, future candidates, and alumni contributors.
- Host families: families involved in welcoming inbound teachers.
- Rotary leaders: club and district leaders stewarding the program.
- Coordinators: people managing exchange logistics, history, submissions, and relationships.
- School administrators: partner school staff documenting participation and impact.

## Core Product Areas

- Public bilingual site shell.
- Living history timeline.
- Story archive with approved teacher, host family, and Rotary leader stories.
- Alumni directory and "where are they now" profiles.
- School and Rotary club profiles.
- Registration and profile onboarding.
- Contributor workflow for stories, classroom projects, curriculum resources, and media.
- Digital curriculum repository for lesson plans, training modules, and ESL instructional videos.
- Replication toolkit for other Rotary clubs.
- Future host-family matching, dashboards, and impact reporting maps.

## Historical Anchors

- 1996: Ralph and Armida Hight initiate the exchange in Rotary District 5680.
- 1996-2001: Mexico era, including the first 13 Wichita teachers sent to Mexico.
- 2001-present: Panama and Argentina expansion, including 39 Panamanian and 45 Argentinian inbound teachers.
- 2010: coordination transitions to Dalia Hale and Shelli Kadel.
- 2020-2023: COVID-19 hiatus and successful inbound-program resumption in 2023.
- 2026: RITE approaches its 30-year legacy with a digital archive platform.
