> *Status*: Complete — produced as Bold in a Day's mock/practice engagement (`bold-tool-plan.md` §14, E2). **Prairie Comfort Mechanical is a fictional client, invented to iterate the Finance-flavor delivery kit before any real pilot — not a real business.**

## Intent

Feature tier because this is the business's first written plan — a new durable artifact everything after it (the chart of accounts, the fractional check-in) references.

## Company Snapshot

- **Legal structure**: Kansas LLC, taxed as S-corp
- **Industry**: Commercial & light-industrial HVAC/mechanical contracting
- **Revenue lines**: 3 — Install & Replacement, Service & Repair, Maintenance Contracts
- **Headcount**: 14 — owner/founder, part-time office manager/bookkeeper, dispatcher, 11 field techs across 3 crews
- **Current state**: QuickBooks Online in place but blended — job materials and labor land in one catch-all account, no revenue-line separation, job costing lives in an outside spreadsheet the office manager rebuilds every month. No written plan; priorities reset with whatever job just landed.

## Mission & Offerings

Prairie Comfort keeps Wichita-area commercial buildings' mechanical systems running, three ways: **Install & Replacement** (new-construction and full-system-replacement bids), **Service & Repair** (break-fix calls, billed time and materials), and **Maintenance Contracts** (recurring seasonal inspection agreements, billed on a fixed schedule).

## Org & Roles

- **Dale Whitfield (Owner)** — sales, estimating, crew scheduling, final sign-off on all bids over $15K
- **Office Manager (0.6 FTE)** — QuickBooks Online, AP/AR, payroll prep, the informal job-cost spreadsheet
- **Dispatcher** — daily crew routing, service-call intake
- **11 Field Techs** — 3 crews (2 install, 1 service); no formal crew leads named yet

## Near-Term Roadmap

Horizon: 12 months

| Priority | Owner | Review Date |
|---|---|---|
| Stand up a 4th crew (service-focused) to clear the maintenance-contract backlog without pulling techs off install jobs | Dale Whitfield | 2026-10-01 |
| Separate job costing by revenue line in QuickBooks so a bid's actual margin is knowable without the spreadsheet rebuild | Office Manager, with Bold-generated chart of accounts | 2026-08-15 |
| Name a lead on each crew so scheduling doesn't route through Dale for every call | Dale Whitfield | 2026-09-01 |

Primary goal (backbone principle 1 — exactly one): **Stand up the 4th crew.** The other two rows exist to make that decision measurable, not to compete with it.

## Where Fractional Help Fits

Once job-cost-by-line is live in QuickBooks (row 2), a **monthly fractional check-in** (`bold-tool-plan.md` §14, tier 1) is the right next step — enough cadence to sanity-check margins on the 4th-crew ramp-up without paying for weekly presence the business doesn't yet need.
